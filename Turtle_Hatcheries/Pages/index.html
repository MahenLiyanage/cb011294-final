Sri Lankan Turtle population


Sri Lanka is an essential nesting ground and habitat for numerous species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. These turtles are essential to the health of marine ecosystems because they keep seagrass and jellyfish populations in control. However, the devastation of nesting grounds caused by coastal development, pollution, and erosion threatens their ability to continue existing. Poaching and unpermitted egg collection have a negative effect on their population, and accidental capture in fishing gear can be hazardous, especially during mass nesting events. Climate change and rising sea levels pose a threat to their breeding habitats. Turtle conservation and rehabilitation is the objective of conservation measures such as marine protected zones and turtle hatcheries. Sri Lanka is working diligently to ensure the long-term survival of its cherished turtle population by promoting responsible tourism and other environmentally conscious practises.



Turtle Hatcheries in Sri Lanka



WHAT is a Hatchery?


A facility devoted to the preservation and protection of sea turtles is known as a hatchery. In this location, turtle eggs are gathered from shoreline nesting locations that are at risk and moved to a controlled setting for incubation. A turtle hatchery's primary objective is to raise the survival rate of sea turtle hatchlings, who are exposed to several dangers in their natural environments. WHY Hatcheries?


The protection of Sri Lanka's unique flora and fauna is of the utmost importance to the country, as it is recognised as one of the most important centres of biodiversity in the world. The problem is addressed by the turtle hatcheries that can be found sprinkled along the western coastline. These hatcheries invest in the continued existence of Sri Lanka's turtle population & following are some of the important benifits it has on turtles.

  1. Nesting Site Protection: Hatcheries provide a safe environment, shielding turtle nests from predators and human interference, ensuring higher survival rates.
  2. Enhanced Hatchling Success: Hatcheries replicate ideal conditions, increasing hatchling success compared to nests in the wild.
  3. Rehabilitation: Hatcheries serve as medical centers, supporting injured turtles' recovery before releasing them back into the ocean.
  4. Research and Education: Hatcheries contribute to scientific research, studying turtle populations and behaviors, while raising awareness through tours and programs.
  5. Conservation and Population Growth: Hatcheries protect eggs and release hatchlings, helping turtle populations and maintaining marine ecosystem balance.
  6. Sustainable Tourism: Hatcheries attract visitors, supporting local economies and promoting responsible practices, vital for hatchery operations.

MISSION

to take preventative steps, conduct research, educate the public, and involve the community in protecting and conserving sea turtles and their ecosystems. We are committed to adopting successful conservation measures and encouraging responsible care of coastal habitats in order to ensure the survival and sustainable populations of sea turtles.

VISION

to develop a world free from risks and injury brought on by humans, where sea turtles flourish in their natural environments. We aspire to be a leading turtle hatchery that actively contributes to the preservation of sea turtle species, fosters environmental awareness, and inspires local communities and visitors to become guardians of marine biodiversity. We are committed to creating a lasting legacy for future generations by working to create a harmonic balance between human activity and sea turtle protection.
