Bentota Turtle Hatchery


Bentota Turtle Hatchery, located in Sri Lanka, is dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles. It has a long and illustrious history, and today it is used to gather and incubate eggs of endangered turtles, providing a nurturing habitat for them until they are released. The hatchery engages communities and provides education to visitors, promoting awareness about the need of sea turtle conservation. It has a beneficial effect on local people, helps to expand turtle numbers, and safeguards breeding grounds. The hatchery encourages environmentally friendly practises and tourism as a means of protecting the future of sea turtles. Guests gain a newfound appreciation for these wonderful animals as they learn about the conservation initiatives that are being made.



Turtle Species Present


  • Olive Ridley Turtle
  • Leatherback Turtle

Other Activities


Bentota Beach

Bentota Beach on Sri Lanka's south-west coast is famous for its golden sands and clean waters. The beach is peaceful and relaxing, surrounded by palm palms and rich vegetation. Visitors can snorkel, jet ski, dive, or relax on the beach under the tropical heat. Bentota Beach is a beach lover's heaven with its scenic beauty and variety of activities.

Bentota River

Sri Lanka's coastal town of Bentota is enchanted by the Bentota River. Visitors can relax as the river flows through tropical mangrove forests and lush sceneries. Boat tours and cruises let visitors explore the calm waters, see wildlife, and enjoy the scenery. Bentota River offers a relaxing getaway into nature, generating lasting experiences for those who canoe, birdwatch, or watch the sunset.

Bentota Sea Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Sea Turtle Protection Association in Sri Lanka protects and conserves sea turtles. The organisation protects endangered turtles by managing nesting beaches and hatcheries. They protect sea turtle eggs, monitor nesting, and release hatchlings into the water. The organisation educates locals and visitors about sea turtle protection. Their passion and proactivity help preserve sea turtles in the region for future generations.
