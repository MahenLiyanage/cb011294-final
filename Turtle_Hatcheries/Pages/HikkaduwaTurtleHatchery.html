Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka, is a renowned facility for the protection and preservation of sea turtles. The facility has a lengthy history spanning decades and has played a crucial role in the conservation efforts for these magnificent creatures. It's primary objective is to provide a secure environment for sea turtle embryos and hatchlings. The hatchery safeguards embryos that would otherwise be susceptible to predation or human interference. Once the baby turtles have hatched, they are meticulously raised in tanks until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. In addition to its hatchling conservation programme, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery actively promotes sea turtle conservation awareness and education. Visitors to the hatchery can learn about the various species of Sri Lankan sea turtles, their life cycles, and the dangers they face.This Turtle Hatchery has had a significant impact on sea turtle conservation in Sri Lanka as a result of its efforts. It has contributed to the increase in turtle populations, protected nesting sites, and raised public awareness of the significance of preserving these remarkable animals. The hatchery serves as a model for responsible tourism and sustainable practises, inspiring tourists from all over the globe to support sea turtle conservation efforts.

Activities


Hatchery Visits

Visitors can explore the hatchery and learn about the different species of turtles being cared for. They can observe the eggs, nests, and hatchlings in various stages of development. This provides an opportunity to understand the challenges faced by sea turtles and the conservation efforts in place.

Egg Collection and Relocation

During the nesting season, visitors may have the chance to participate in the collection of turtle eggs from vulnerable nests along the beach. These eggs are then carefully relocated to the hatchery to protect them from predation and other threats. This hands-on experience allows visitors to actively contribute to turtle conservation.

Releasing Hatchlings

The hatchery often organizes hatchling releases, where visitors can witness the magical moment of releasing the baby turtles into the ocean. This activity not only promotes awareness but also gives visitors a sense of fulfillment and a direct involvement in helping the turtles start their journey.
